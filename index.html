Hello, I am Dale Riechert, a professional engineer and transplant to the beautiful Seattle area.

Through my dad, another engineer, I was exposed at an early age to the personal computers of the time and dial-up internet, seeding a lifelong passion for technology and a full appreciation for the computing revolution that has come. I heeded the opportunity to study Informatics at Arizona State University, an interdisciplinary field spanning areas of computer science, computer information systems, and human computer interaction. Fundamentally, I seek to bring creative solutions to the problems that unfold between computers, data, and people—as we head deeper into the 21st century.

On the side I enjoy video games, film, music, graphic design, photography, hiking, traveling Japan, animals, and collecting charmingly analog watches, with which I have also learned the essentials of watchmaking.

Light Through Matter

 This media installation combines a MIDI keyboard with a projector so as a pianist plays colorful shapes are projected onto the cloud suspended above them. The keyboard note's octave determines the color, and tempo determines the speed of movement and distortion. I created the Max patch that the system runs on while also assisting in construction and installation of the equipment.
Video • Source Code

Computer Interface Feedback Through Haptics

 This project aimed to explore brain plasticity and sensory replacement by replacing the computer mouse cursor with vibration feedback. The cursor's X and Y position, as well as the color of the item the cursor was positioned over, were each mapped to a vibration motor. The system was built with an Arduino, Max, and an HTML/CSS webpage on a laptop computer.
Photos • Report Paper • Source Code

Vulgar Phone Cases

As a group project, we had to utilize data representing vulgarity in the fabrication of some surface. We chose to use the occurances of swear words in our text messages and then mapping that data onto 3D-printable phone cases. The phone case models were loaded into Rhinocerous 3D, with the data mapping occuring in Grasshopper. I created this Grasshopper definition for the rest of the group to use.

Letter Frequency Keyboard

This was an individual project in which I took a similar approach as the Vulgarity project above. Using 3D models of keyboard keycaps, I modified each of them using Grasshopper based on each letter's frequency in the English language before having them 3D printed and arranged.
