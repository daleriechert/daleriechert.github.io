Hello, I am Dale Riechert, a recent graduate of informatics and digital culture at Arizona State University.

Informatics is an interdisciplinary field incorporating areas such as computer science, information systems, and human computer interaction. I am what comes between data, computers, and people. As we head deeper into the 21st century these computer systems are playing an ever bigger role, becoming essential to nearly every other field out there. Digital culture is the study of digital media and the digital systems in our everyday life, bringing creative solutions to today and tomorrow's problems.

My passions include the video game and consumer technology industries. I also hold interests in graphic design, photography, foreign culture, film, and more. As a hobby I like to collect, mostly Russian and mechanical, wristwatches.

Light Through Matter

This media installation combines a MIDI keyboard with a projector, so as the pianist plays, shapes are projected onto the transparent fabric suspended above them. The note's octave determines the color, and tempo determines the speed. I created the Max patch that the system runs on while also assisting in construction and installing of the equipment.

Computer Interface Feedback Through Haptics

This project aimed to explore brain plasticity and sensory replacement by replacing the computer mouse cursor with vibration feedback. The cursor's X and Y position, as well as the color of the item the cursor was positioned over, were each mapped to a vibration motor. Vulgar Phone Cases

As a group project, we had to utilize data representing vulgarity in the fabrication of some surface. We chose to use the occurances of swear words in our text messages and then mapping that data onto 3D-printable phone cases. The phone case models were loaded into Rhinocerous 3D, with the data mapping occuring in Grasshopper. I created this Grasshopper definition for the rest of the group to use.

Letter Frequency Keyboard

This was an individual project in which I took a similar approach as the Vulgarity project above. Using 3D models of keyboard keycaps, I modified each of them using Grasshopper based on each letter's frequency in the English language before having them 3D printed and arranged.

Motion Control Drum Machine

This is an interactive drum machine using an Arduino and potentiometers for note adjustment, a Wii remote and GlovePIE for motion control, and Max for note processing and system communication. Each time the user swings the remote, in the order indicated by the LEDs, one of the six notes will play.
